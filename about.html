The Story Behind the Scout

Meet Ruben den Uyl

The Strategic Maker

I’ve always been fascinated by how things fit together—whether it's Legos in my childhood home in Odijk or complex organizational strategies in a boardroom. I call myself a Strategic Maker.

In a world that often separates the "thinkers" from the "doers," I believe the most effective progress happens when you bridge that gap. Pocket Pioneering was born from this belief: that strategy is only as good as the tools you build to execute it.

The Journey

From Odijk to Delft

Growing up in a small village meant 30km of cycling every day to school. It wasn't always fun as a teenager, but it built a resilience and a connection to the landscape that stayed with me. At TU Delft, I studied Architecture, where I realized my true talent wasn't just in designing buildings, but in the strategy and management behind them.

The Vancouver Perspective

Moving to Canada was a defining chapter. Transitioning from Rotterdam's urban density to Vancouver's wild nature, I worked as a Business Analyst in Renewable Energy. Operating in remote indigenous communities taught me that the "Canada mindset"—resourcefulness, respect for the environment, and pioneering Spirit—is what modern businesses need most.

A Maker's Heart

When I’m not solving organizational puzzles, you’ll find me in my workshop or out in the mountains. I build furniture, I tinker with gadgets, and I tour-ski into the heart of the wilderness.

"The feeling of freedom and connection to nature is what gives me direction. My goal is to help preserve the 'playgrounds' we have—both in nature and in our creative professional lives."
